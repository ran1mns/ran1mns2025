Cours Anglais – 30 Septembre 2025

Temps et formes

  • Present simple + be ...ing
  • Prétérit simple + be ...ing
  • Futur simple + be ...ing
  • Les + ...ing : temps plus complexes, pas forcément utiles au quotidien

Verbes réguliers

  • Ajouter ed pour former le prétérit : played, cooked, eliminated
  • Prononciation :
    • played → le ed est muet
    • cooked → ed prononcé t
    • eliminated → ed on prononce le e
  • La base verbale ne change pas

Verbes irréguliers

Pattern : trois formes

  • sleep → slept → slept
  • feel → felt → felt
  • to break → broke → broken
  • Première forme : infinitif → I want to break
  • Deuxième forme : prétérit → I broke my car
  • Troisième forme : participe passé / adjectif → This table is broken
  • Exemple notable : to go → went → gone
  • Verbe to be : I was, you were

On last Saturday morning, I woke up at 7.30am.

Anecdote embarrassante

I used to buy my beers at a Turkish grocery on the corner of my street.
Every day, a man, who looked old to me, gave me my beers and took my money.
One day, I didn't have enough money (20 cents), so he said it's ok and invited me to come back the next morning with the money.
The next day, he wasn't there. A wife welcomed me and asked, "What do you need today?"
I ordered a beer and mentioned I needed to pay more because the boss let me go the previous day without paying.
She looked at me strangely and asked: "The boss, who?" 
I was embarrassed and said: "You know, the man who was here yesterday, maybe your grandfather…"
She replied, "He's my husband."
