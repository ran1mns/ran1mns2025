Titre du Cours : Prétérit et autres temps en anglais

Introduction

Le prétérit est un temps essentiel en anglais pour parler du passé. Il est crucial de bien le maîtriser pour communiquer efficacement. Ce cours vous guidera à travers les bases du prétérit, ainsi que d'autres temps simples et complexes en anglais.

Concepts Clés

Présent Simple + be ...ing

Le présent simple avec la forme en "be ...ing" est utilisé pour parler d'actions en cours de réalisation au moment présent.


I am eating an apple. (Je mange une pomme.)

Prétérit Simple + be ...ing

Le prétérit simple avec la forme en "be ...ing" est utilisé pour parler d'actions en cours de réalisation dans le passé.


I was eating an apple. (Je mangeais une pomme.)

Futur Simple + be ...ing

Le futur simple avec la forme en "be ...ing" est utilisé pour parler d'actions qui seront en cours de réalisation dans le futur.


I will be eating an apple. (Je mangerai une pomme.)

Temps plus complexes avec ...ing

Les temps plus complexes utilisant la forme en "...ing" sont souvent moins utilisés au quotidien mais sont importants pour des conversations plus sophistiquées.

Prétérit des verbes réguliers

Pour les verbes réguliers, il suffit d'ajouter "ed" à la base verbale. Par exemple :


play - played - played
cook - cooked - cooked
eliminate - eliminated - eliminated

Prétérit des verbes irréguliers

Les verbes irréguliers suivent des schémas spécifiques. Par exemple :


sleep - slept - slept
feel - felt - felt
break - broke - broken

Exemples notables :


go - went - gone
be - was/were - been

Utilisation du prétérit dans une phrase

Pour utiliser le prétérit dans une phrase, vous pouvez suivre cette structure :


I went to [lieu] to [action].
Exemple : I went to the restaurant to eat a delicious pie.

Exemple de récit au prétérit

Voici un exemple de récit utilisant le prétérit :


On last Saturday morning, I woke up at 7.30am. It was after a very good night and just after an hour listening to music. On weekends, I like waking up slowly with classical music! I chose to listen to France musique because it's a public radio and there's no advertising.
I took my clothes and went to the bathroom. Like every morning, I had to pee… Then I had a shower.
The soap I used was a special one, with no dirty components like paraben or other ones.
I dried myself with a brand new and fresh lavanda smelling towel! I dressed myself and went to the kitchen to prepare my breakfast.
My coffee is a maxhavelaard labeled coffee, I try to do things ethically!
So I put the coffee beans in the blender, then with a spoon of chicoree, I poured the mixed coffee in my italian coffee maker.
I took some bread slices and put some marmelade on them. I'm sure there's a better word to write this but I don't remember it. It's a word like the french sentence: "culture is like marmelade, the lesser you have the more you __…__ it" (It's to spread)
With a glass of water, I ate my marmeladed bread! I drank my coffee, I cleaned the table and washed the dishes.
On Saturday, I usually have to fill my fridge, so after I wrote a list, I drove to the supermarket to buy some groceries.
This time it was pretty fast, I went there early enough so there were almost no customers. I bought some cheese, chose a new carpet for my kitchen, I took beers of course, and many other things!
The problem was the highway. There was a congestion so I arrived only at 10 am at home. I put what I bought in the right place, again, I know there's a word more appropriate but I don't remember it.
After this, I prepared another coffee and had a break watching TV. I watched a documentary about an old and sad story : the solar temple order
It ended just before the time to prepare lunch!

Anecdote embarrassante

Voici une anecdote utilisant le prétérit :


I used to buy my beers at a Turkish grocery. It was just in the corner in the street I lived in.
Every day, a man, who looked old for me, gave me my beers and took my money.
One day, I had not enough money, it was something about 20 cents, so he said to me that's it's ok and invited me to come back the next morning with the money.
But the next day, he wasn't here. A wife welcomed me and asked me "what do you need today", as usual I ordered a beer and I added that I need to pay more because the boss let me go the passed day without paying all my beers.
She looked strange at me and asked : "The boss, who?" I was embarrassed and did not know what to say, so I decided to say, "you know the man who was here yesterday, it's your grandfather maybe…" She looked at me stranger than before and said : "he's my husband"

Exercices Pratiques

  1. Écrivez une phrase au prétérit en utilisant un verbe régulier.
  2. Écrivez une phrase au prétérit en utilisant un verbe irrégulier.
  3. Racontez une courte histoire au prétérit en utilisant au moins cinq verbes différents.
Corrigé 

1. I played football yesterday.
2. I went to the park last weekend.
3. Yesterday, I woke up early and had breakfast. Then, I went to the park to play football with my friends. We played for two hours and then went home. I was very tired but happy.
